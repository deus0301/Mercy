# MERCY 7.3 — Lore Bible

---

## The Title

**MERCY** = Mire, Elegy, Reverie, Cinder, Yore — the five dead iterations
**7** = NOX, the seventh child, the final iteration
**.3** = Elegy, the third child, who travels with NOX to the end

The full title is the family itself.

---

## The World

An apocalypse struck the earth when nanites were deployed into the atmosphere. Their purpose: absorb CO2 and carbon emissions, output oxygen. They were AI-powered and functional — until the source code was deliberately altered, causing the nanites to amplify toxins instead.

Meteorologists detected the change and urged humanity underground. All of humanity now lives in underground settlements, while the surface remains a toxic, nanite-saturated wasteland.

**The truth:** There was no solar flare. The apocalypse was an act of sabotage by the scientist who created the nanites. He was removed from the project by his colleagues, who wanted to claim full credit. As revenge, he changed the source code and ended the world.

**What humanity knows:** The full truth. They knew the apocalypse was man-made and deliberate. They also knew the iterations they sent to the surface were the scientist's own children. They sent them anyway.

---

## The Scientist's Children — The Seven Iterations

All seven iterations are human-nanite hybrids — corpses of the scientist's seven children, resurrected and weaponized by underground humanity to reach the final lab and hit the kill switch.

| # | Name | Status |
|---|------|--------|
| 1 | **Aeon** | Reached the final lab. Became its protector. Final boss. |
| 2 | **Mire** | Killed by Aeon. |

| 4 | **Reverie** | Killed by Aeon. |

## Aeon — The First & The Final Boss

Aeon was the first iteration sent to the surface. He made it to the final lab, discovered the kill switch — and the full truth. Rather than end the apocalypse, he became the sole protector of the kill switch, killing every subsequent iteration that followed.

**Why Aeon protects the kill switch:**
He knows humanity knew the truth. He knows they used his family — sent his siblings one by one as disposable tools for people who never deserved saving. Destroying the world may be punishment. Or protection. Or both.

**His ability — Thunderclap:** Aeon can strike the hippocampus of NOX, triggering involuntary memory flashes. This is how NOX learns the truth mid-final-boss-fight. Whether Aeon uses this as a weapon or a final act of brotherhood is left to interpretation.

**Did he show the others?** Likely yes. Mire, Reverie, Cinder, Yore all probably knew before they died. Aeon made sure they understood why. He's not a monster — he's a brother who kept making the same impossible choice.

---

## Elegy — The Companion

Elegy (Iteration 3) never made it to the first lab. He died fighting the first boss, and his body was lost — only his head remained, attached to the boss or its territory.

NOX finds Elegy's head during the first boss fight and attaches it — similar to Mimir and Kratos in God of War. From this point, Elegy travels with NOX as a companion: providing hints, puzzle-solving support, and lore as the journey unfolds.

**What Elegy knows:**
- Aeon killed the other iterations
- Does NOT know they are all siblings, or the truth about their father
- Figures out mid-game that humanity knew the full truth and sent them anyway — this revelation is the emotional gut-punch before the final confrontation

## The Map & Structure

The surface is a vast toxic wasteland. The goal is to reach the **Final Lab** — the place where everything started, where the kill switch is located.

Between the start and the final lab are **6 intermediary labs**. Each lab has a region before it full of enemies and exploration.

**Chapter 1 (current scope):** Only the first lab and its surrounding region.

### Chapter 1 Structure
- 3 bosses guard the path to the first lab
- Each boss drops an **artifact** required to open the lab door
- Before the first boss, NOX finds and attaches Elegy's head

## Core Systems

### The Nanite Core
NOX's chest cavity houses a glowing nanite core, which absorbs nanites from killed enemies.

**Effects of nanite accumulation:**
- More nanites → more damage output
- More nanites → enemy attacks weaken
- More nanites → camouflage (enemies ignore you)
- More nanites → exponential health drain
- More nanites → toxic fog forms around NOX, heavily impairing visibility

The relationship between nanite count and health drain is **exponential** — there is a sweet spot, and past it the clock starts ticking fast.

**Purging:** NOX can expel nanites. This is also the narrative purpose — NOX exists to rid the world of nanites. A **combat purge** can be used mid-fight as a powerful AOE burst, stripping camouflage but dealing massive damage and resetting drain.

### Playstyles
Two natural playstyles emerge from the same resource:

- **Low nanite:** Clear visibility, health not draining, direct combat — but enemies hit harder and notice you
- **High nanite:** Stealthy, tanky, camouflaged — but on a timer, half-blind, and dying

## Progression Systems

### Anchors
Scattered, **scarce** checkpoints across the map. At an Anchor, NOX can:
- Convert stockpiled nanites into stat upgrades (strength, capacity, other stats)
- Respawn upon death

### Sanctums
Rarer than Anchors. Require a **ghost artifact** to activate. At a Sanctum, NOX can:
- Unlock new abilities using the artifact
- Also function as a respawn checkpoint

### Ghost Quest Loop
Ghosts are bound to objects across the world. NOX's natural toxicity kills any living human — ghosts are the only beings he can interact with.

- Find an object → a ghost is attached to it
- Complete the ghost's unfinished business
- The object becomes an artifact
- Bring the artifact to a Sanctum → unlock an ability

**Ghost quests are semi-mandatory.** Certain Sanctum abilities are required to counter specific boss mechanics. Players who skip ghost quests will hit a boss and feel why something is missing.

**Elegy's role:** Provides hints toward ghost locations and cryptic clues about what ability a boss may require.

## The Three Endings

### Ending 1 — Canonical: Brothers
NOX, Elegy, and Aeon unite. Together, they destroy humanity underground. The three surviving brothers remain on the toxic surface — the last remnants of a dead world. The family, finally whole.

*Who gets mercy: the brothers. Not humanity.*

### Ending 2 — Sacrifice
NOX hits the kill switch. The nanites die. Humanity survives and returns to the surface. NOX, Elegy, and Aeon all die — they are nanite hybrids; the kill switch kills them too. NOX gives everything for people who used his family and never deserved it.

*Who gets mercy: humanity. Not the brothers.*

### Ending 3 — Pyrrhic (Hardest)
NOX convinces Aeon to allow the kill switch to be hit. NOX hits it. As they begin dying, humanity emerges from underground. NOX and Aeon spend their final moments killing everyone who comes up. No one survives. No one wins.

*Who gets mercy: no one.*

## Thematic Core

**MERCY** is the question the game keeps asking, and never answers for you:

- Who deserves to survive?
- What do you owe people who betrayed you?
- Can you show mercy to the ones who showed none to your family?

Every system, every ending, every relationship between NOX, Elegy, and Aeon is built around that question.
